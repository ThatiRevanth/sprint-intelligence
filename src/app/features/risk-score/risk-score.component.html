🧠 Sprint Risk Score

How is the Risk Score calculated?

The sprint risk score is a health score from 0 to 100. A higher score means the sprint is healthier. It's based on work item counts (not story points) and is a weighted average of four factors:

FactorWeightWhat it measures
Completion Progress35%Percentage of items done vs how much time has passed. If 50% of time is gone but only 20% of items are done, this score drops.
Time Balance25%Whether remaining time is enough for remaining items. Penalizes when many items are left but little time remains.
Blockers25%Ratio of blocked/stale items to total items. More blockers = lower score.
Velocity Trend15%Compares current sprint's completed item count to the average of the past 3 sprints.

Risk Levels

70–100 On Track  40–69 At Risk  0–39 Likely to Slip

Example

Mid-sprint: 50% time elapsed, 6 of 20 items done (30%), 2 blockers, velocity matches past average.

FactorScoreWeighted
Completion (6/20 done ÷ 50% time)6060 × 0.35 = 21.0
Time Balance7171 × 0.25 = 17.8
Blockers (2/20)8080 × 0.25 = 20.0
Velocity Trend100100 × 0.15 = 15.0

Total: 74 → On Track

Tips

• The score updates in real time as work items change state.
• "Blockers" include stale items (not updated in 2+ days), blocked dependencies, and pending PRs.
• Velocity compares completed item count from the last 3 sprints as baseline.
